Keftedes

What is Keftedes?

Greek meatballs are everybody’s favourite Greek traditional food, whether it’s on a meze platter at a kids party or at a friends & family gathering at a Greek taverna. The name comes from Persia, where “kufte” -meaning ground meat- travelled all around the Middle East, Turkey and the Balkans. One of its forerunners in ancient Greece was called “myma”, made with minced meat or fish combined with many herbs and spices.

Ingredients

  • Pork
  • Beef
  • Onions
  • Stale bread
  • Eggs
  • Parsley

Steps

  1. Grind the pork and beef, mix, and add seasonings.
  2. Pre-heat a skillet to medium heat, add 2 tbsp of oil, and place a 1/2 cup of onions for 3 minutes.
  3. Add mixed meat concoction and let cook for about 4 minutes on each side.
  4. Serve onto place and top with a generous amount of chopped parlsey!
